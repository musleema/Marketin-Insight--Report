SUPERSTORE ANALYSIS REPORT

INTRODUCTION

The report's purpose is to analyze Supermarket data to identify key factors that can provide actionable insights for proper strategies. The analysis will focus on identifying trends and correlations within the data to point out potential Supermarket drivers.

Data Overview: the data set contains records of 9994 and 13 columns in all, there are no data type mismatches. The dataset covers sales, customer segments, locations, and profitability. The data set consists of categorical and numerical variables;

  • Categorical Variables:
    • Ship Mode, Segment, Country, City, State, Region, Category, Sub-Category
  • Numerical Variables:
    • Sales, Quantity, Discount, Profit, Postal Code.

Initial Data Exploration Results

  • Duplicate records found: 17
  • Sales made:
    • Ranges from $0.44 to $22,638.
    • The median sale value is $54.49, meaning most sales are relatively small.
  • Quantity:
    • Most orders have 2 to 5 items, but some go up to 14.
  • Discounts:
    • Discounts vary, with a maximum of 80%.
    • The median discount is 20%, meaning discounts are common.
  • Profit:
    • Profits range from -$6,599.98 (loss) to $8,399.98 (gain).
    • Some transactions result in major losses.

Key Insights Identified

  1. Top-Selling Products (by total sales):
    •  Phones: $330,007
    •  Chairs: $327,777
    •  Storage: $223,843
    • Binders: $203,409
    •  Tables: $206,965
  2. Most Profitable Categories:

       Technology: $145,454 (highest profit)

       Office Supplies: $122,364

Furniture: $18,421 (least profitable)

Conclusion

The analysis of the Sample Superstore dataset reveals that Phones and Chairs drive the highest sales, while Technology is the most profitable category. However, 1,869 transactions resulted in losses, likely due to high discounts. Pricing and discount policies should be adjusted to avoid such losses and to enhance overall profitability and business performance.
